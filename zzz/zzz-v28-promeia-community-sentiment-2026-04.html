Sentiment Verdict

Comments on adjacent ZZZ posts show pragmatic skip intent: multiple ID users citing Ramiel and v3.x meta characters as pull targets. This is triage, not backlash.

Globally, the design controversy has deepened rather than resolved. The Apr 12 back outfit change was received more negatively on TikTok than the original reveal. Top-liked comments: "ruined*" (1,655 likes), "thats so boring wth" (1,750 likes). The anchor framing "ice Evelyn" scored 1,461 likes on the initial reveal and has settled as the default community shorthand.

For campaign planning: voice acting and the fusion lore concept are praised independently of design — these are the angles to amplify. The first two weeks face maximum pull-budget competition: NTE launches Apr 29, WuWa v3.3 Apr 30, GI v6.5 ends May 19.

KOL Talking Points
Lead With
Two-Souls Lore Concept
"Two personalities, one body — she carries both. The story actually pays it off."

Most praised aspect across all platforms regardless of design stance. Works without game knowledge.

"Ice Evelyn" Framing
"Evelyn but Ice — the community coined it, and it fits."

Organic comparison to beloved v2.5 character. Adopting it feels authentic, not forced.

Gameplay Accessibility
"Strong even without her signature weapon."

Directly addresses the "team is expensive" concern in ID community.

Design Controversy as Hook
"They changed her design twice due to fan pressure — which version did you prefer?"

Lean into discourse as engagement. Controversy drives eyeballs; don't suppress it.

Address Proactively
  • Design controversy → Frame as "which side are you on?" — engagement beats suppression.
  • Team cost → Show accessible team compositions. PityGamersRoom's kit guide already does this for ID.
Avoid
  • Comparing Promeia favorably to her original design — mourning crowd is large and vocal.
  • Leading with gacha rates — budget sensitivity signalled in ID comments.
  • Comparing Promeia to Cissia — different audience, different controversy.

Interesting Facts

Cosplay companies producing Promeia costumes 3 weeks before banner launch. Spotted on X/Twitter Apr 16, before any official cosplay partnership.

Why it matters: Third-party cosplay activation before launch = strong organic virality signal. Coordinate official cosplay shoutouts around May 6 to amplify existing momentum.

The highest-liked comment on the initial reveal was PRO-Promeia (2,621 likes). "I must be too old of an adult cause I see everyone complain about her look but I fuck with it." Despite vocal design-dislike discourse, the most liked comment defended the original aesthetic.

Why it matters: The silent majority may be more design-positive than the noise suggests. Content speaking to this group is an underserved angle with large existing reach.

"Promeia Getting MOGGED by Billy" (97K views) was posted March 28 — before any design changes. The Billy pull competition is structural, not driven by design backlash.

Why it matters: Design fixes alone won't close the Billy gap. Promeia needs a distinct pull narrative (lore, accessibility, new archetype) that complements Billy rather than competing head-to-head.
