Sentiment Verdict

The Indonesian community has not yet mobilized around Promeia at 18 days pre-banner. Facebook ID — the primary ID hub (70K members) — returned zero dedicated Promeia posts. Comments on adjacent ZZZ posts show pragmatic skip intent: multiple ID users citing Ramiel and future v3.x meta characters as pull targets. This is triage, not backlash — the ID audience is rationing a finite budget across a crowded 2026 calendar.

Globally, the design controversy has deepened rather than resolved. The Apr 12 back outfit change was received more negatively on TikTok than the original reveal. Top-liked comments on the change video: "ruined*" (1,655 likes), "thats so boring wth" (1,750 likes), "Now it's boring. Her old one was pretty unique" (487 likes). The community's anchor framing — "ice Evelyn" — scored 1,461 likes as the top comment on the initial reveal and has now settled as the default shorthand.

For campaign planning: Promeia's pull intent will recover if gameplay and story content convert the design-disappointed segment. The official Agent Record post (HoYoLab, 136K views, 2.9K likes) confirms strong appetite for official character content. Voice acting and lore (the fusion concept) have been praised independently of design — these are the angles to amplify. The first two weeks of the banner face maximum pull-budget competition: NTE launches Apr 29, WuWa v3.3 Apr 30, GI v6.5 ends May 19.

KOL Talking Points
Lead With
Two-Souls Lore Concept
"Two personalities, one body — she carries both. The story actually pays it off."

Most praised aspect across all platforms regardless of design stance. Works without game knowledge.

"Ice Evelyn" Framing
"Evelyn but Ice — the community coined it, and it fits."

Organic community comparison to a beloved v2.5 character. Adopting it feels authentic, not forced.

Gameplay Accessibility
"Strong even without her signature weapon — not a whale-only character."

Directly addresses the "team is expensive" concern surfaced in ID community.

Design Controversy as Hook
"They changed the design twice because fans had so many opinions — which version did you prefer?"

Lean into the discourse as engagement. The controversy drives eyeballs; don't suppress it.

Address Proactively
  • Design controversy → Frame: "Her look split the community — which side are you on?" Engagement > suppression.
  • Team cost / new archetype → Show her in accessible teams. PityGamersRoom's kit guide already does this for ID.
Avoid
  • Comparing Promeia favorably to her original design — the mourning crowd is large and vocal.
  • Leading with gacha rates or wishing meta — "team is expensive" framing signals budget sensitivity.
  • Comparing Promeia to Cissia — different audience, different controversy, different outcome.

Interesting Facts

Cosplay companies are already producing Promeia costumes — 3 weeks before banner launch. Spotted on X/Twitter Apr 16, before any in-game release or official cosplay partnership announcement.

Why it matters: Third-party cosplay activation before launch = strong organic virality signal. Coordinate official cosplay community shoutouts around banner launch to amplify this existing momentum.

The highest-liked comment on the initial reveal was PRO-Promeia (2,621 likes). Despite vocal design-dislike discourse, the most liked single comment said: "I must be too old of an adult cause I see everyone complain about her look but I fuck with it. It looks cool on her + Matches her vibe."

Why it matters: The silent majority may be more design-positive than the noise level suggests. Campaign content that speaks to this group — people who liked the original aesthetic — is an underserved angle with large existing reach.

"Promeia Getting MOGGED by Billy" is the most-viewed community post at 97K views — and it was posted March 28, before any design changes. The Billy pull competition predates the design controversy entirely; it is structural.

Why it matters: Design fixes alone will not close the Billy gap. Promeia needs a distinct pull narrative (lore, accessibility, new archetype) that complements Billy rather than competing with him head-to-head.
